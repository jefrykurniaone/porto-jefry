<objective>
Fix the two responsive layout bugs that remain after the Phase 9 navbar shipped: (1) the hero profile photo and heading get clipped at the top behind the `position: fixed` navbar on short viewports — phones and especially landscape/short-height tablets (LAYOUT-01); and (2) a horizontal page scrollbar appears somewhere between 320px and 1024px wide (LAYOUT-02). Both fixes live almost entirely in `src/app/globals.css`, with one className change in `Hero.tsx` and co-located test assertions. This is a single coordinated plan because both bugs touch the shared `globals.css` file and the same hero region — splitting them would force a sequential wave on one file with no parallelism gain.

Purpose: After Phase 9 settled the navbar at its de-facto 5rem (80px) height, the hero's initial layout still never accounts for the fixed header, and no root-level horizontal-overflow containment exists. This plan introduces a single canonical `--navbar-height` token, makes the hero clear that height, root-causes the overflow culprit (the `min-width: 11.5rem` hero CTAs in the 513–1024px band), and adds a defensive `overflow-x: clip` guard.

Output: Updated `src/app/globals.css` (new `--navbar-height` custom property; hero top-clearance; root overflow containment; widened fixed-width CTA guard), a one-line className change in `src/components/sections/Hero.tsx`, and new behavior assertions in `src/components/sections/Hero.test.tsx`.
</objective>
